Life Currently


I'm currently the Data Analyst at Amazon, and just made 1.5 years in my role. I love what I do but I've started to feel stagnant and I am ready to grow. Amazon supports their associates in growing and pay for schooling all the way up to a Bachelor's Degree. So, I took advantage of this opportunity and recently just finished a 5-month Software Developer Bootcamp. I've decided to continue pursuing my dream and will be enrolling into college next Spring or Summer to obtain my Master's Degree.

Black Women in Technology


Before everyone even knew of the movie, Hidden Figures, I was already following and ready to break into the STEM workd solely because of learning about Katherin Johnson. In my high school math class, I was assigned a project and chose her because of her historic findings and doings in the STEM world. I was even more intrigued when I found out about her personal history and her life story inspired me so much. I started doing research on STEM degrees and programs and her sororitys' history as well, whcih I did eventually join in college.

What sparked my interest in computers?


In elementary school, I was always into sports, dancing, and singing. I was a honors student and have always loved learning, so school was naturally fun for me. One day, in my computer class I started clicking around and became very interestsed in what there was to explore and learn about them. I took up a typing class and joined both FBLA and DECA where my knowledge grew with business and computers. I stopped attending recess and would spend my extra time learning about technology and all the ins and outs with it.
